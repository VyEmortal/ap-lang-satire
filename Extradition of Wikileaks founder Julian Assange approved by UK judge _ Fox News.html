        Florida Man Leads Healthcare Reform

        By Anders Hagstrom | Fox News
        • Facebook
        • Twitter
        • Flipboard
        • Comments
        • Print
        • Email
        close
        NEWYou can now listen to Fox News articles!

        Macclenny, Florida — Skyrocketing healthcare costs are a defining characteristic of life in the United States. Today, 16.9% of Americans have some financial barrier to health care. The average cost of a three-day hospital stay in the U.S. is $30,000, and even private health insurance does not even come close to covering this price. One Florida man, Jacob Jackson, 44, recently experienced this reality. His mother, 71, died last week after falling down a set of stairs, and Jackson had to make the tough decision not to get her treatment because of the high cost. Ever since, Jackson has been emotional.

        Macclenny, Florida — Jacob Jackson displaying his patriotism by leading his campaign.

        “The first few days were rough,” he said, “but now I realize that my mother’s death shows respect for our country’s system.” Jackson praised the American healthcare system for allowing his mother to die, stating that the hospital did nothing wrong and acted in accordance with the beauty of American capitalism.

        “This is how things are supposed to work in a democracy. No one needs a bunch of old people draining our hospital’s resources so that young, rich people have to pay ridiculously high costs,” said Jackson. “Those stairs were definitely in my favor,” he added.

        Jackson and his family have celebrated this loss by hosting a barbeque party in their backyard. He invited 48 family members — all of whom showed up. But neighbors have filed complaints with the Macclenny Police Station due to the noise. “They’ve been blasting fireworks all day, and at 3 a.m. those knuckleheads were still going,” claimed a local resident. Another household claimed that its children were traumatized from the gunshots firing. Macclenny Police have declined to report on the complaints.

        Jackson has further embraced his civic duties by launching a campaign to combat these rising healthcare costs. Jackson is firmly opposed to big government plans to institute universal health care or cost caps, preferring a much more economical, creative solution that does not infringe on the wallets and liberty of American citizens.

        His campaign, “Make Healthcare Great Again,” appeals to socially conscious young people to take action. This is a simple, efficient solution, and it is a disgrace that legislators have yet to implement it. “It’s really just one action, and then you’re done,” explains Jackson. “Reach out to your elderly relatives — grandparents, parents, aunts, uncles, anyone — meet up with them and push them down the stairs. With them dead, they won’t have those slow, drawn-out health struggles that drain our hospital systems.”

        Jackson's campaign poster advocating for people to kill their mothers in an act of patriotism.

        This elegant proposal relies on simple supply and demand: with fewer illness-prone elderly people, the demand for healthcare decreases, contributing to lower prices.

        “And as a bonus, no more annoying old relatives whining at you to ‘get a job’ or ‘move out of my basement; you’re 44,’” adds Jackson.

        Jackson’s campaign has already begun attracting national attention. Congresspeople from Florida, Texas, Alabama and Mississippi have expressed support and appealed to their constituents to take part. Senator Ned Truz, one of the most enthusiastic proponents of “Make Healthcare Great Again,” declared: “I shoved my mom down the stairs just yesterday. Right before her skull cracked on the concrete, I saw in her eyes the gratitude for this opportunity to contribute to lowering health care costs for future generations!”

        High healthcare costs affect millions of people in the United States. The determined efforts of one man to change this system shows the true strength of our country. More information about how to get involved in this campaign can be found at www.makehealthcaregreatagain.com.
